🧠 Neural-Trail Confidence Engine — Methodology (fully transparent, no black box)
1. Setup Score (0–100): a weighted composite of Trend (EMA20/50/200 stack, ADX/DI, Supertrend), Momentum (RSI triangular zone centred on 55, MACD histogram sign+slope, Stochastic), Volume (RVOL, 5-day surge trail, OBV trend) and Price-Action (bullish engulfing, hammer, marubozu, inside-bar breakout, HH-HL structure, gap-up-on-volume). A BUY setup requires Setup Score ≥ 60, ATR% between 1.0%–8.0% and 20-day avg turnover ≥ ₹3 Cr (liquidity floor). The identical rule is applied bar-by-bar across all 5 years of history to generate the backtest trades below — today's signal is validated by, not separate from, the historical test.  |  2. 5-Year Backtest: every historical day the rule fired, a trade is simulated — entry at the next session's open (no look-ahead), stoploss at the nearest confirmed fractal swing-low support minus a 0.3% buffer, target fixed at 1:3 risk-reward, exit at whichever of stoploss / target / 10-day time-stop comes first (a same-day stoploss+target hit is conservatively scored as the stoploss). Only one open trade per stock at a time.  |  3. Confidence Level (0–100%): a transparent single-layer weighted-and-sigmoid-squashed score (technically a 1-layer neural/logistic scoring function — not a deep-trained black box) blending today's Setup Score, the backtest win-rate after a Wilson-score shrinkage for sample size (so 3 lucky trades can't outrank 40 consistent ones), the raw trade count, whether the nearest resistance leaves realistic room for the fixed 3R target, live RVOL confirmation, and today's price-action strength. Weights are fixed and fully disclosed: bias=-3.0, setup=+2.2, winrate=+2.0, sample=+1.0, rr_realism=+1.0, volume=+0.8, pattern=+0.6.  |  Reading the win-rate: at a fixed 1:3 risk-reward, the mathematical break-even win-rate is only 25% (win% × 3R = loss% × 1R). A stock showing a 45–60% historical win-rate here is not a weak signal — it implies a materially positive expectancy per trade before costs, which is why Confidence Level weighs the win-rate alongside sample size and setup quality rather than by itself.
